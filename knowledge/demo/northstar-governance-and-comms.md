# Northstar Incident Governance and Communications

> Fictional evidence pack for the ONF product demonstration. Qualified local counsel and real organizational policy must govern an actual incident.

## Decision authority

The incident commander coordinates the response but cannot waive clinical safety controls or authorize ransom payment alone. The clinical lead owns diversion and patient-safety acceptance. The recovery lead owns technical integrity evidence. Legal counsel and the privacy officer own notification analysis. Executive leadership owns extraordinary financial decisions.

For the tabletop, the named roles are:

- Elena Torres — legal, privacy, insurer, and external coordination lead.

## Evidence preservation

Preserve affected logs, alerts, ransom notes, system times, network records, and forensic images. Do not wipe affected systems or reconnect isolated segments merely to accelerate recovery. Record who approved each containment and restoration action, the evidence used, and the time of approval.

## Notification and compliance

The team must notify qualified legal counsel, the privacy officer, and the cyber insurer promptly under the fictional response plan. Whether a regulator, patient, partner, or law-enforcement notification is legally required depends on confirmed facts and jurisdiction. The team must not state that data was stolen or that no breach occurred until evidence supports the claim.

## Communications rhythm

Publish an internal situation update every thirty minutes containing only confirmed facts: patient-safety status, affected services, containment status, recovery evidence, current decision, owners, and next checkpoint. Do not speculate about attribution. Public communications require approval from the incident commander, clinical lead, and legal counsel.
